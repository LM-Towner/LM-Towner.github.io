MobileMonday Presents: Mobile, Bots and API with IBM

June 5, 2017

I spent the weekend at a Hackathon hosted by IBM. At first I didn't know what to expect but it was a lot of fun. The event was from June 2, 2017 - June 4, 2017. On Friday was the general meet and greet and formation of teams. I decided to form a team with familar faces and pitch and idea that I had been working with solo. We decided to make an application for tackling that age old question "What should we eat?"

We spent the entire Saturday working alongside each other. We split the team up into two. We had the front-end team(2pp) working on the UI design using React Native. I personally worked on the back-end,constructing the server(expressjs), database(MongoDB) and supplying all the endpoints for the team. We had one additional teammate who also flipped flopped in between the two teams to offer a fresh set of eyes whenever one of us got stuck.

On Sunday it was judgement time, we didn't feel comfortable with our app, but we had to present something. I decided that since I had work so hard with the back-end I would try to at least try to win something. To our surprise we actually won the prize of a "Best use of Loopback", which essentially a prize for my implentation of the back-end portion.

Winning this prize was super affirming because it allowed me to pat myself on the back and see how much I had grown

Conquering my Weakness'

May 20,2017

Math Algorithms are my kryptonite. I knew coming into the tech industry that I'd eventually have to get better at them. Most companies nowadays require you to perform some degree of these lovely problems. However I want to clarify why they are my kryptonite, it’s not that I don’t like math. It’s quite the opposite, I love math, I love puzzles, and I like brain teasers.But when presented with an algorithm such as ‘find the sum of all primes below two million Project Euler I find myself wondering why would I ever want to do that? I personally love real world problems and solving things dealing with real life applications. If you want to fully gauge how a person thinks give them creative range to solve a problem. For me these math algorithms place you in a box to only solve it a few ways. Of course there are many ways to solve it , loops and methods etc. but at the end of it all, it’s all the same.

Now with that said, all algorithms are not same. Algorithms are used to test how a person problem solves, so I do, understand the necessary reason for using them. During my free time in the last few days, even though I they are not my favorite, I’ve been on this website called project Euler working what i’ve considered my weakness. According to the website each problem should only take you a few minutes to figure it out. For me it takes about 30minutes or more to solve these problems. I think the reasoning behind this is because I already told myself that I dislike these, problems. It’s really hard to commit yourself when you’re already t old yourself you don’t want to do it.

My goal for this week is to do a few problems a day until I am comfortable with them, and then once I am comfortable, I will try to go for speed. The first step was admitting that this is my weakness, the next step is to conquer it.

“To share your weakness is to make yourself vulnerable; to make yourself vulnerable is to show your strength.”Criss Jami

Algorithms

May 22,2017

I started doing some of these problems on Project Euler. The biggest issue I find myself is figuring out whether or not I want to use Ruby or Javascript. When I use Ruby I tend to run to the built-in methods that do the harder work for you. Such as the Enumerable documentations for solving problems that requiring adding or to aggregate a collection. When I use Javascript, I find myself using less of built-in methods and trying to write it as cleanly as possible. When I use JS, my code feels like it goes on for many lines whereas I can get to a simple solution with Ruby in just a fewer lines. Before I type it up, I start of my solutions on a whiteboard or even a notebook. I want to try to get into the habit of thinking it through without my text-editior yelling at me about syntactic errors. Once I finish I upload and see if the problems works. So far, it's only been a few syntactic errors but for the most part I have the general idea of how to solve the problems.

Creating a Social Media Clone

May 6, 2017

In the last post, I discussed sharpening the skills I learned at DBC. So what better way to do that then to build a Ruby on Rails application. From past experience in group projects, I have always dealt with the backend portions of the application. I have always done one or two things, the database setup or the Route Setups. So with this project I wanted to expand and practice on my front-end designing. Some of my cohort-mates used Materialize in our past projects, which worked out great, but since I wanted to mimic the way twitter looked like I decided to use Bootstrap instead. Using bootstrap wasn't as bad as I'd expected. It took about an hour to familiarize myself with it's documentation. And few more to actually use in a way that made sense to what I was trying to do. Overall building this social media clone was good practice to exercise my front-end skills. I look forward to working on future projects using it and even exploring other frameworks.

Life after Dev Bootcamp (DBC)

April 28, 2017

So it's been about two weeks since I finished Dev Bootcamp. During phase 3 (which is the last 3 weeks of the program) I was counting down the days, I couldn't wait to stop looking at the computer for 12+ hours and just relax. It turns out, I do not know how to relax.Relaxing scares me, quiet scares me, and if I go a few hours without my computer I get a little a "crazy". During these last few months, I've grown an attachment to consistently working non-stop until I drop and waking up and repeating again. DBC provided me with a structure and all I had to do every day was show up do my chores for the day, get stuck in the land of confusion, get found (hopefully), and create some awesome applications.

Now I'm on the other side of the grass, which I thought was greener, I'm finding it harder to just accept that I no longer have the structure that was provided for me. It is now my job to create "structure" and to continue to experience a Growth Mindset I start my day between 7 & 9 am, I take my dog for a walk, go to the gym and I try to dedicate at least 5 hours a day actively learning. My plan of attack is to sharpen those skills I've learned at DBC while learning something new. Every day I log on to Team Treehouse and pick a track to focus on. My goal this week is to get better at my front-end skills so that I can become a well-balanced web developer
